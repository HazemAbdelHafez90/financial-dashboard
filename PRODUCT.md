# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Hazem — a single power user managing his personal finances daily on mobile. Future state: anyone who wants a clean, capable personal finance tracker without the bloat of mainstream apps. The primary context is a quick mobile check-in: how am I tracking this month? Secondary context is deeper desktop analysis.

## Product Purpose
Personal finance command center: track expenses, monitor budgets, analyze spending habits, and watch net worth grow. Success is a user who opens this app instead of a spreadsheet and feels genuinely in control of their money.

## Brand Personality
Sharp, considered, personal. Precision without corporate distance. The tool of someone who cares about their money and trusts their own judgment.

## Anti-references
- Generic fintech apps (Mint, YNAB's UI): white, teal, overly friendly, watered-down
- Crypto dashboards: neon on black, animated price tickers, visual noise
- SaaS cream / Notion clones: off-white, neutral-gray, no character
- Banking app corporate blue: trust theater, not actual clarity

## Design Principles
1. **Data is the hero.** Numbers should be instantly readable. Never bury the figure in decoration.
2. **Mobile-first, desktop-worthy.** Designed from 320px up, but earns its keep on a large screen too.
3. **Personal scale.** One user, one financial life. The UI should feel tailored, not mass-produced.
4. **Progressive depth.** Home gives a snapshot. Tabs give detail. Nothing forces complexity on the user.
5. **Future-proof identity.** Design choices should hold when this becomes a product for many — not just a personal dashboard.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA minimum. Numbers must remain legible under color-blind conditions (not color-only status signals). Support reduced motion. Touch targets ≥44px on mobile.
